Roy G. Biv and Crypto Mirror have taken some screen shots for this quick tutorial, the pictures are attached inline in this message. We'll show you how to use CryptoMirror to have a conversation with a friend over AOL's OSCAR using Apple's built-in Messages client.


Generating an Identity

Create an identity to communicate with. Press the Me tab, type in a name for the identity, and click Generate New Identity button. Please note that the name of the identity will be sent as plaintext. 

This is the view "Crypto Mirror" has after a new IM from "Roy G Biv" has arrived.



Initiating a conversation
There's a bit of a chicken and egg action with starting a conversation in that the underlying cryptographic protocol achieves both authentication and confidentiality of messages. So to start things off, the first sender has to know the receiver's public key. 

Press the "copy pubkey to clipboard" button and paste that over if the crypto pal you'd like to talk to does not have your identity's public key yet.

In the screenshot below "Roy G Biv" has sent over their public key to "Crypto Mirror". Hover the mouse over the blob to get the public key. 




Sending Messages

Now that "Crypto Mirror" has the public key for "Roy G Biv", "Crypto Mirror" can start sending messages to "Roy G Biv".

To send a message: 
1. Select the send tab and select the Crypto Pal to write a message to.
1. Click the 'Select Output Channel' button, and then click on the text entry field to send commands to. (Try this a second time in case it doesn't work)
2. Type the message in the send view and hit 'return' to send the message. The message should automatically be inserted into the entry field that was selected and a return key should also be sent, making the message arrive.



In the screenshot below, two messages have been sent to "Roy G Biv" and a third is on the way.



Reading Messages

To read a message, just hover the mouse over the crypto mirror blob the conversation partner has sent over and the plaintext message will be displayed in the Read View.
